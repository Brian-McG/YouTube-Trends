Introduction

YouTube is a probably the most popular video-sharing service in the world: 300 hours of video are uploaded every minute (according to their stats). Of those that achieve views in the billions, the great majority are music videos. We wanted to compare how the popularity of the five most watched YouTube videos changed over time, how they spread and how different countries compared to each other. We decided to use data on YouTube searches, comments on the videos and Google searches as a proxy for that popularity.

Data

While all the data we used is easily accessible, there is unfortunately no single place where it is bundled. We used a combination of custom code and open source libraries to download and get the data in a format we could use. This involved querying Google Trends for each song, country and source combination we needed and then parsing the YouTube comments for every video.

Initial Design

In our initial design that we did during the class presentation we had set it up so that two videos can be chosen and compared at the same time. The whole motivation for our design was comparing two separate things hence the reason for splitting the design down the middle so the two videos could be easily compared. A overall problem was the fact that the design was not very appealing, which we would have to focus on.

At the top of the design was the selector where you select the two videos using the drop downs that you want to compare. Then information such as the title, view count, dislike count, like count, comment count, date published and next to all that an embedded video for the selected video was displayed for that video. The visualization also incorporated being able to select the type of trend i.e do you want to see the youtube trends for that visualisation or the google trends for it? This could also be selected using the drop downs next to the video names.The problem with this part of the visualisation was the fact that it was quite boring as there was a lot of text and numbers used and no symbols so it meant that there would be a lot of reading involved making it less appealing.

Below the top section of is the map section. The map section shows depending on the deepness of red the normalised popularity of a video for each country after a specific number of weeks after release of the video. The maps information is linked to a slider below it that allows you to adjust the number of weeks after the video was released allowing you to compare the two different videos over a period of 52 weeks after their initial releases and also allowing you to be able to see the change in popularity for a specific video over a certain amount of time. We chose to do weeks after release rather than the actual time frames due to the fact that some of the videos are released up to 2 years before other ones. This is again in aid of being able to compare between the videos. The map also would allow for zooming to allow for easy selection for smaller countries as well as a tooltip on hover over of the countries giving the normalised popularity value not just for the video you selected but also for the video you are comparing it to. The comments we got back on this was it was too difficult to tell the difference of popularities between certain ranges as our colour gradient was too similar.

The last part of our initial design is the graphs that show the popularity of the selected video over time i.e it will display the number of comments, the number of google searches and the number of youtube searches over the 52 week period after the release. The graphs would also allow a you to select the type of data i.e you may want to see only google trends and then the lines representing this data would stay and the rest of the lines would be greyed out,a merge function was also mentioned that would allow you to compare the two separate videos in one graph. The graphs also had a vertical black line that is linked to the slider that moved to the week selected in the slider, making it easier to relate the data from the map to the graph.The issues that were mentioned with this section wa that the greyed out lines were difficult to see and also we only use one colour in the separate graphs making it hard to pick out what represents what and also the fact that no texture was used.
